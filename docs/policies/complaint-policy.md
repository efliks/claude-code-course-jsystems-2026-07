# Complaint Policy (Warranty Claims for Defective Goods)

Version 1.0 — example document for the Hardware Service Decision Copilot MVP.

## 1. Warranty period

1.1. Products are covered by a **24-month warranty** from the date of purchase.
1.2. Complaints submitted after 24 months from the purchase date are **rejected** under this policy.
1.3. If the purchase date cannot be verified and the declared date is within the warranty period, the claim is assessed on the declared date, noted as unverified.

## 2. Coverage

The warranty covers defects attributable to manufacturing or materials, including:
2.1. Hardware failures under normal use (device does not power on, component failure).
2.2. Display defects: dead/stuck pixels above the ISO class threshold (more than 3 defective pixels), backlight bleeding, flickering.
2.3. Battery defects: swelling, or capacity below 80% within the first 12 months.
2.4. Manufacturing flaws present at purchase (misaligned housing, loose ports not caused by force).
2.5. Recurring defects after a previous repair of the same fault.

## 3. Exclusions

A complaint is **rejected** when the defect results from:
3.1. **Mechanical damage caused by the user**: drops, impacts, pressure (e.g. cracked screens or housings with impact marks).
3.2. **Liquid damage**: corrosion, triggered liquid-contact indicators.
3.3. Normal wear and tear: cosmetic scratches, worn keycaps, gradual battery aging within spec.
3.4. Unauthorized repair, modification, or opening of the device.
3.5. Use contrary to the manual (wrong power supply, extreme environments).
3.6. Software issues not caused by a hardware defect (misconfiguration, third-party software, malware).

## 4. Burden of proof and ambiguity

4.1. Within the first **12 months** from purchase, a defect is presumed to have existed at purchase unless evidence shows user-caused damage.
4.2. After 12 months, the customer must plausibly show the defect is not caused by usage or mishandling.
4.3. Where photo evidence is inconclusive about the cause, a decision requires additional evidence (better photos, description of circumstances) before approval or rejection.

## 5. Remedies and process

5.1. Approved complaints are resolved in this order of preference: **repair**, then **replacement** if repair is not feasible within 14 days, then **refund** if neither is feasible.
5.2. The company responds to a complaint within **14 days** of submission.
5.3. An approved complaint includes shipping instructions for sending the device to the service center at the company's cost.
5.4. Rejected complaints include the reason and, where applicable, a paid repair offer.

## 6. Evidence requirements

6.1. The customer must describe the defect and the circumstances of its discovery.
6.2. The customer must provide at least one clear photo showing the defect or the device's condition.
6.3. Claims of invisible defects (e.g. "battery drains fast") are assessed on the description and purchase date; the photo must still show the device to confirm identity and absence of exclusion criteria (3.1–3.2).
